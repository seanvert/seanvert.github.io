# flow


<a id="org42f6e36"></a>

## phenomenology of enjoyment


<a id="orgf41f9b1"></a>

### 1

the experience usually occurs when we confront tasks we have a chance of
completing


<a id="orga35e286"></a>

### 2

we must be able to concentrate on what we are doing


<a id="org73706fe"></a>

### 3/4

concentration is usually possible because the task undertaken has clear goals
and provides immediate feedback


<a id="orgd860a30"></a>

### 5

one acts with a deep but effortless involvement that removes from awareness the
worries and frustrations of everyday life


<a id="orgf1f7503"></a>

### 6

enjoyable experiences allow people to exercise a sense of control over their
actions


<a id="org45a296c"></a>

### 7

concent for the self disappears, yet paradoxically the sense of self emerges
stronger after the flow experience is over


<a id="org2e9fc4f"></a>

### 8

the sense of duration of time is altered; hours pass by in minutes, and minutes
can stretch out to seem like hours.

## the effects of the family on the autotelic personality


<a id="org6dd9ac8"></a>

### strength, happiness, satisfaction is tied to 5 characteristics

1.  clarity

    unambiguous goals and feedback from what is expected of their parents

2.  centering

    focus on what they're doing rather than the results of those experiences

3.  choice

    variety in possibilities

4.  commitment

    trust that allows the child to feel comfortable enough to set aside the shield
    of his defenses, and become involved in whatever he is interested in

5.  challenge

    parents' dedication to provide increasingly complex opportunities for action to
    their children

<a id="orga31e497"></a>

## the power of dissipative structures


<a id="orgb4cb180"></a>

### how people turn a hopelessly situation into a flow one

1.  unselfconscious self-assurance

    implicit belief that their destiny was in their hands
    
    tries to do his best within the system in which he must operate

2.  focusing attention on the world

    constantly processing information from their surroundings
    
    check if their personal goals are fit and relevant to external events

3.  discovery of new solutions

    -   focus attention on the obstacles and move them out of the way
    -   focus on the entire situation, and think about if alternative goals are ok
